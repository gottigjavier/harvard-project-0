Pa-Kua and Wuxing.

In martial art, both the eight states of change (Pa-Kua) and the five elements (Wuxing) are used as combat patterns as for opponent profiles.



Pa-Kua as Pattern

The translation of the Chinese words, Pa means ‘eight’, and Kua ‘mutations’, ’transformations’ or ‘changes’. Each of the eight states of change is represented with three lines, and can be unbroken or broken, yang or yin (⚊ or ⚋). According to legend, it was the mythical chinese emperor Fu-Hi who saw the existence of similarities between the marks inscribed on the shell of a turtle on the shore of the Yellow River and the constellations of the sky. From this observation, created the eight trigrams, which govern the universal laws, the order of the world, the phenomena of nature, and the human being. Each trigram is in relation with one of the eight cardinal points.

There are many ways to order trigrams. Here we will show the form of opposites faced and the familiar pattern.

Following clockwise:

Opposite Faces.
  • CH'IEN: Sky - Father
  • SUN: Wind - Eldest Daughter
  • K'AN: Water - Middle Son
  • KEN: Mountain - Younger Son
  • K'UN: Earth - Mother
  • CHEN: Thunder - Eldest Son
  • LI: Fire - Middle Daughter
  • TUI: Lake - Younger Daughter
Family Pattern.
  • CH'IEN: Father - Sky
  • K'UN: Mother - Earth
  • CHEN: Eldest Son - Thunder
  • SUN: Eldest Daughter - Wind
  • K'AN: Middle Son - Water
  • LI: Middle Daughter - Fire
  • KEN: Younger Son - Mountain
  • TUI:Younger Daughter - Lake
